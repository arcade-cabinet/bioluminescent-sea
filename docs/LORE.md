# Lore

The trench is not a setting. It is a character. Every creature, every
pirate lantern, every landmark is there because the trench wants
something from the diver — a witness, a map, a glance. The game never
says this aloud. It shows it through silence, through light, through
the way the abyss gets quieter the deeper you go.

## The Cartographer's Guild

The player belongs to a disbanded-but-not-really cartographers' guild
whose job was, for centuries, to *witness* unseen water. They never
published their maps. The last surviving member of the guild left a
note that read:

> *"A trench you cannot see remembers every light that was ever lowered
> into it. Be seen. Be gentle. Bring the map back up."*

The submersible carries one of the guild's brass sextants. You never
see it; it's on a shelf behind the pilot. But its absence is why the
dive ends by surfacing calmly rather than breaching.

## The Four Biomes

- **Photic Gate** — the last place surface daylight matters. Plankton
  mats here still remember the sun. The guild used to call it "the
  vestibule" because it was the only floor of the trench you could dive
  without breathing equipment.
- **Twilight Shelf** — an accidental shelf left by a basalt slide a
  few thousand years ago. Anglers colonized it first; their lantern
  rods are how the guild learned the trench could be read by light.
  Pirates set up lantern-skiff routes along the shelf to farm dive
  crews. Most of them are dead; some of the lanterns are not.
- **Midnight Column** — the water column above the trench proper. No
  floor in view in any direction. Eels live in the column by forgetting
  where up is. This is where the oxygen budget stops being a curiosity
  and starts being an argument.
- **Abyssal Trench** — the trench floor, ~3600m down. Here the only
  color is bioluminescent mint and (rarely, and only close to the
  Living Map) warn-red. The Living Map is a place, not a creature;
  the guild left it there on purpose.

## Creature taxonomy (short version)

- **Plankton** — not individual animals; one plankton *bloom* is one
  creature in the engine. Scored small because they are common, but
  they're how a diver learns to steer.
- **Jellyfish** — bells. The game's central brand image. A jellyfish
  lights its own bell in a two-beat rhythm; a diver reading the rhythm
  can predict the drift.
- **Fish** — schooling silverlight fish. Worth more because they
  startle; a collected fish triggers the rest of its school to
  scatter — which is why collecting fish is often where the chain
  multiplier spikes.
- **Anglers** (threat) — predator by definition, but the guild's
  records call them "librarians," because they hunt by listening for
  careless movement.
- **Eels** (threat) — fast. No lore; they are just fast.
- **Pirate lanterns** (threat) — human threat, not animal. A lantern
  cone on the water is an intrusion the trench does not tolerate.

## The codename

Every dive is a *place name*, not a player name. Three words
(adjective-adjective-noun) drawn from the guild's nomenclature pool:

- First adjective: quality of water / light / mood
- Second adjective: material / color / artifact
- Noun: geography / fauna / instrument

"Kelpish Vellum Benthos" is a trench. "Glacial Lapis Caldera" is a
trench. The diver is the unnamed thing; the codename is the trench.
That's the correct emphasis.

## Route landmarks

The six named landmarks (Kelp Gate, Lantern Shelf, Whale-Fall Windows,
Trench Choir, Abyss Orchard, Living Map) are guild waypoints. They are
*real places* in the dive's depth column — you pass Kelp Gate on the
way down and then it's above you. The Living Map at 3200m is the end
of the authored chart. Diving past it on a successful run triggers a
small flourish on the completion screen — a line from the guild's
closing note.
